Learn Russian with Short Stories (2): Max Reads Grin

Topics: learning  -- Keywords: Aleksandr Grin  literature  stories  Russian With Max 

Зелёная лампа (The Green Lamp) is a short story from 1930 by Aleksandr Grin (1880-1932), the surname being an abbreviation. Regarding Aleksandr Grinevski (Russian: Александр Гриневский), Wikipedia offers a real recommendation: “His stories mainly revolve around the sea, adventures, and love.”


Read!

Зелёная лампа tells the story of a beggar who, in exchange for money, must keep a lamp burning for several hours each day and stay by it. What seems like a cruel prank or humiliation turns out to present opportunities. If you just read.


Reading in Russian

Russian With Max has a series where short stories are read and explained: Reading in Russian. Some episodes include, besides the Introduction and Part 1 (51 m), Fat and Thin by A. Chekhov (2019, 63 m) and Princess Frog | Wisdom in Fairy Tales (2019, 59 m). See also Reading Russian Contemporary Literature | A Short Story About Coffee (42 m).

In the February 2019 episode, The Green Lamp by A. Grin (60 m), Max reads Grin’s famous short story. You can also order a bilingual audiobook of The Green Lamp via Max’s website, supporting his work and keeping his lamp lit. Definitely worth checking out!

Perfective and imperfective

This often comes as a setback for students of Russian: of (almost) every Russian verb there are two. Which do mean approximately the same thing, but express very different things. So you need to know both, and of both learn the conjugations.
